# Hybrid SOFC–Turbofan Aeroengine Integration (FlyEco Project)

Developed and evaluated hybrid-electric aeroengine integration concepts combining Solid Oxide Fuel Cells (SOFC), gas turbines, hydrogen storage systems, thermal management systems, and electrical power distribution architectures for sustainable aviation applications.

The project focused on the conceptual design and comparative analysis of two hybrid propulsion integration configurations:

* Hybrid SOFC–Turbofan Engine
* Partially Nacelle-Integrated Hybrid Propulsion System

Worked on:

* System-level analysis of hybrid-electric propulsion architectures
* Evaluation of thermal management and hydrogen integration strategies
* Numerical and analytical assessment of propulsion-system feasibility
* Investigation of power-distribution and energy-management concepts
* Comparative analysis of integration trade-offs involving efficiency, weight, safety, and structural constraints
* Technical documentation, engineering literature review, and concept evaluation

Engineering Areas:
Hybrid-Electric Propulsion | Sustainable Aviation | System Modelling | Thermal Management | Hydrogen Technologies | Fuel Cell Systems | Aeroengine Integration | Engineering Analysis
